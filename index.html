LENS PRESET
Hold the phone in portrait. The value above is the lens's FOV across its wide sensor axis — the figure manufacturers publish (e.g. a main camera ≈ 78°). In portrait that wide axis runs top-to-bottom on your screen, so it becomes your vertical on-screen view; the narrower left-right view is computed from it and the video's aspect ratio. Pick the lens, set the matching preset — if it's wrong the overlay won't line up. When unsure, Main ~78° is the safe choice.
MOTION TRACKING
SENSOR SOURCE
Gyro (recommended) tracks rotation with the gyroscope only — very smooth, no compass jumps, drifts a few degrees over several minutes. Compass adds the magnetometer for true-north lock but jumps if there's magnetic interference (metal, magnets, some cases). Auto prefers Gyro. Since you set 0° with the shutter, Gyro is usually best. Use the diagnostic.html page to test your sensors.
How it works: point your phone at where you want the panorama to start, then tap the amber shutter. That frame defines direction 0°. Reticles appear for the remaining targets — align reticle to target, hold steady ~½ second, auto-capture fires. Keep the phone vertical and rotate around your own axis, not the phone's.

This preview is a pose-only reprojection — each frame is placed from its sensor azimuth/elevation/roll assuming a perfect rectilinear lens. Residual misalignment is normal: it comes from lens distortion, FOV estimate error, and parallax, none of which can be solved without feature matching. For a clean stitch feed the frame zip into Hugin or PTGui — metadata.json gives each frame's yaw/pitch/roll/FOV as a starting point, and they solve distortion and align on image features.
